## 0. Scope Control

This document defines the implementation specification for upgrading the current Product Import Center and Product Library foundation into the Product Intelligence Center.

This task does not authorize implementation.

Do not perform the following in this specification task:

- Do not modify application code.
- Do not create database migrations.
- Do not create APIs.
- Do not change frontend UI.
- Do not change existing business workflow.

Future implementation must follow the V5.3 master development guideline:

- Existing business modules remain the source of truth.
- AI output is advisory by default.
- AI may create recommendations, drafts, analysis records, mappings, logs, and review records.
- AI must not silently overwrite Product Library, pricing, Quote, PI, or customer records.
- All paid AI execution must pass through AI Cost Control.
- Supplier cost and purchasing data must not be exposed to unauthorized roles.

## 1. Current Problem Analysis

### 1.1 Current State

The current system already has a strong reusable foundation:

- Product Library / PIM
- Product categories
- Product attributes
- Product variants
- Product media and documents
- Product Intelligence fields
- Product Knowledge Engine
- Supplier Excel / CSV import
- Embedded image extraction
- Import batches and drafts
- Draft approval into Product Library
- Product Price Engine
- AI Cost Control
- Quote Builder and PI Builder

The Product Import Center can parse supplier spreadsheets, extract product rows, group variants, extract images, create product drafts, and approve them into Product Library.

However, the current design is still closer to an import utility than a full Product Intelligence Center.

### 1.2 Main Problems

#### A. Import format is not fixed

Different suppliers may provide different input structures:

- XLSX
- CSV
- Product catalogs
- Product images
- Bilingual Chinese / English spreadsheets
- Multi-row headers
- Merged cells
- Embedded images
- Different product and variant grouping conventions
- Different naming for the same field

Examples:

| Supplier Field | Standard Field |
| --- | --- |
| Model | SKU |
| Product No. | SKU |
| Item Code | SKU |
| 型号 | SKU |
| 规格 | Variant Size / Dimensions |
| 单价 | Supplier Cost or Price |

The system cannot depend on a fixed CSV template.

#### B. Product data must become standardized

Supplier data is messy. Product Library must remain clean.

After import and review, data must be standardized into:

- Product Master
- Dynamic attributes
- Variants
- Media
- Commercial data
- Source traceability
- AI product profile

#### C. Current review experience is not enough for long-term product intelligence

Long vertical review cards are not suitable for real product operations.

The target UI should work more like a practical B2B product management backend:

- Fast list scanning
- Image-first review
- Variant grid editing
- Confidence badges
- Missing data warnings
- Source data traceability
- Bulk review actions

#### D. Product knowledge is spread across several foundations

Current product knowledge exists across:

- `products`
- product variants
- product attributes
- product knowledge terms
- product relationships
- media assets
- price rules
- import drafts
- AI content drafts

The Product Intelligence Center should not duplicate these records. It should coordinate them and expose clean, reusable product context for AI and sales workflows.

#### E. AI needs controlled access to product context

Future AI modules need product data for:

- Product recommendation
- Customer-product matching
- AI quotation
- AI PI assistance
- AI Sales Assistant
- AI Solution Generator

AI cannot become the source of truth. It should read approved product context and create reviewable suggestions only.

## 2. Target Architecture

### 2.1 Architecture Goal

The Product Intelligence Center should become the standardized product knowledge foundation for Restaurant Setup Pro AI Sales OS V5.3 Lite.

It should transform flexible supplier/product inputs into a clean B2B product intelligence model used by:

- Product Library
- Product Knowledge Engine
- Product Price Engine
- Customer Intelligence
- Opportunity Intelligence
- Sales Intelligence
- Quote Builder
- PI Builder
- Future AI Solution Generator
- Future AI Sales Assistant

### 2.2 Layered Architecture

```text
Supplier / Product Inputs
  - XLSX
  - CSV
  - Product images
  - Product catalogs
  - Future PDF / website sources
        ↓
Import Intelligence Layer
  - Raw extraction
  - Header detection
  - Column recognition
  - Image extraction
  - Product / variant grouping
  - AI-assisted mapping suggestions
        ↓
Human Review Layer
  - Draft review
  - Field correction
  - Variant correction
  - Attribute confirmation
  - Duplicate decision
  - Approval / rejection
        ↓
Product Intelligence Center
  - Product Master
  - Dynamic attributes
  - Variants
  - Media
  - Commercial data
  - Source traceability
  - AI Product Profile
        ↓
Downstream Business Consumers
  - Customer-product matching
  - Quote Builder
  - PI Builder
  - Sales Assistant
  - Solution Generator
  - Website / marketplace later
```

### 2.3 Source of Truth Rules

| Business Data | Source of Truth |
| --- | --- |
| Product master data | Product Library / Product Intelligence Center |
| Product variants and attributes | Product Foundation / PIM |
| Product knowledge relationships | Product Knowledge Engine |
| Supplier cost and reference price | Product Price Engine |
| Product media | Media assets linked to Product Library |
| Quote item snapshots | Quote Builder |
| Customer-facing PI | PI Builder |
| AI execution and budget | AI Cost Control |

AI mapping and AI profile outputs are not final product data until approved.

### 2.4 Product Intelligence Entity Model

The Product Intelligence Center should coordinate these domains:

1. Product Master
2. Product Attributes
3. Product Variants
4. Product Media
5. Commercial Data
6. Source Traceability
7. AI Product Profile
8. Product Quality / Readiness
9. Human Review Records

### 2.5 Product Master

Minimum standardized fields:

- Product ID
- Product Name
- SKU
- Category
- Main Image
- Status
- Visibility
- Updated Date

Product Master is the stable identity of the product.

### 2.6 Product Attributes

Attributes must remain dynamic and category-driven.

Examples:

- Material
- Color
- Size
- Style
- Finish
- Seat material
- Frame finish
- Base size
- Suitable top size
- Packaging size

The system should not hard-code category-specific attributes in business logic.

### 2.7 Product Variants

Variants represent sellable or purchasable differences under one product.

Variant fields:

- Variant ID
- Product ID
- Variant SKU
- Variant name
- Variant image
- Size
- Color
- Finish
- Supplier cost
- Reference price
- Selling price
- Status

Example:

Product: DB-A002 Stainless Steel Table Base

Variants:

- 380×380
- 400×400
- 450×450
- 500×500
- 550×550

### 2.8 Commercial Data

Commercial data should connect to Product Price Engine rather than become a parallel pricing system.

Fields and concepts:

- Supplier cost
- Supplier currency
- Exchange rate
- Margin rule
- Reference selling price
- Manual override
- Price status
- Price updated date

Formula ownership:

```text
Supplier Cost + Exchange Rate + Margin Rule = Reference Selling Price
```

Final deterministic calculation belongs to Product Price Engine.

AI may explain pricing risk but must not silently change price.

### 2.9 AI Product Profile

Each approved product should be able to maintain an AI Product Profile.

The AI Product Profile supports:

- Product keywords
- Suitable industries
- Suitable customers
- Selling points
- Application scenarios
- Recommended use cases
- Not recommended cases
- AI summary
- Sales notes
- Customer matching hints
- Quote/PI readiness hints

AI Product Profile must be reviewable and version-aware.

## 4. API Requirements

### 4.1 API Design Principles

- Reuse existing Product Library and Import Center APIs where possible.
- Add Product Intelligence APIs beside existing business APIs.
- Do not replace current Product Library endpoints abruptly.
- AI endpoints should return drafts, suggestions, mappings, and analysis.
- Final product changes require explicit human approval.
- All AI endpoints must call AI Service Layer and AI Cost Control.
- Role-based field redaction must be enforced server-side.

## 6. AI Service Integration

### 6.1 Integration Principle

All AI-related Product Intelligence work must use the existing AI Business Brain foundation.

Required integration path:

```text
Product Intelligence UI / API
  ↓
runAiAction(...)
  ↓
AI Context Builder
  ↓
AI Cost Control
  ↓
Provider Adapter
  ↓

  ↓
```

Recommended AI action keys:

| Action Key | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `product-intelligence.file.analyze` | Analyze uploaded file structure |
| `product-intelligence.column.map` | Suggest column-to-field mappings |

| `product-intelligence.variant.group` | Suggest product/variant grouping |
| `product-intelligence.category.suggest` | Suggest product category |
| `product-intelligence.duplicate.review` | Explain possible duplicate matches |
| `product-intelligence.profile.generate` | Generate AI Product Profile draft |
| `product-intelligence.matching.prepare` | Prepare product context for customer matching |

### 6.3 Context Types

Recommended context types:

- `product_import_source`
- `product_import_draft`
- `product_master`
- `product_variant`
- `product_attribute_template`
- `product_price_context`
- `product_media_context`
- `product_knowledge_context`

### 6.4 Cost Control Requirements

AI cost control must apply to:

- Batch file analysis
- AI mapping suggestions
- AI attribute extraction
- AI product profile generation
- Product matching preparation
- Quality review

Required behavior:

- Use deterministic/rules parsing first.
- Use AI only when mapping or classification is ambiguous.
- Estimate cost before batch AI analysis.
- Require confirmation if estimated cost exceeds threshold.
- Cache column mapping by supplier/header signature.
- Cache product profile generation by product version.
- Log all AI activity, including mock/rules calls.
- Do not log API keys or secrets.

### 6.5 Provider Strategy

Phase 1 implementation should support:

- Rules provider
- Mock provider

Future implementation may support:

- OpenAI
- Gemini
- Claude
- Qwen

Paid provider usage must require:

- Admin / Owner permission
- `allow_paid_provider = true`
- Budget available
- User confirmation when required
- Provider key configured

### 6.6 AI Output Rules

AI may create:

- Column mapping suggestions
- Category suggestions
- Attribute suggestions
- Product/variant grouping suggestions
- Duplicate explanations
- Product profile drafts
- Missing data warnings
- Customer matching hints

AI must not:

- Create final products without approval.
- Overwrite Product Library data without approval.
- Change supplier cost or selling price silently.
- Expose supplier cost to unauthorized users.

## 10. Risks

### 10.1 Data Duplication Risk

Risk:

Future AI modules may duplicate product, variant, price, or media data.

Mitigation:

- Product Library remains source of truth.
- Product Intelligence stores AI profile and review metadata only.
- Quote and PI use snapshots, not duplicated master data.

### 10.2 AI Overconfidence Risk

Risk:

AI may map supplier fields incorrectly or hallucinate product attributes.

Mitigation:

- Show confidence scores.
- Require human approval.
- Preserve original source values.
- Store mapping reason and review record.
- Use rules first and AI only for ambiguity.

### 10.3 Supplier Cost Leakage Risk

Risk:

Supplier cost and purchasing fields could appear in Sales UI, AI output, or customer-facing documents.

Mitigation:

- Enforce server-side redaction.
- Add role tests.
- Do not include supplier cost in customer-facing AI context.
- Keep cost-sensitive prompts separated from sales/customer prompts.

### 10.4 Incorrect Product Grouping Risk

Risk:

Rows may be grouped into the wrong product/variant structure.

Mitigation:

- Show grouping preview.
- Allow split and merge.
- Preserve source row references.
- Add supplier-specific mapping cache after approval.

### 10.5 Pricing Mistake Risk

Risk:

AI or import logic may create wrong selling price.

Mitigation:

- Product Price Engine owns calculation.
- AI may only flag risk or explain.
- Manual override requires authorized role and audit.
- Quote Builder snapshots final quote price.

### 10.6 Large File Performance Risk

Risk:

Large supplier files may be slow or block the app.

Mitigation:

- Use import job status.
- Add background job processing later.
- Limit AI calls per batch.
- Cache mapping.
- Add import duration metrics.

### 10.7 Media Storage Risk

Risk:

Imported/generated images currently rely on local/public storage patterns.

Mitigation:

- Keep media abstraction through `media_assets`.
- Plan Supabase Storage or Cloudflare R2 migration.
- Avoid long-term large base64 storage in database.

### 10.8 Monolithic Codebase Risk

Risk:

Current backend and frontend are productive but large. More Product Intelligence logic may make maintenance harder.

Mitigation:

- New implementation should isolate services where possible.
- Keep routing thin.
- Add tests for service-level behavior.
- Avoid putting complex mapping logic directly into UI handlers.

### 10.9 PostgreSQL Compatibility Risk

Risk:

Local SQLite compatibility and PostgreSQL production behavior can diverge.

Mitigation:

- Keep migrations as schema source of truth.
- Test additive migrations on PostgreSQL.
- Avoid SQLite-only SQL behavior in new migrations.

### 10.10 Workflow Complexity Risk

Risk:

Adding AI mapping and review could make import slower for users.

Mitigation:

- Keep the core flow simple:

```text
Upload → Analyze → Review → Approve → Product Library
```

- Use sensible defaults.
- Require review only for low confidence or missing required data.
- Support bulk approval for clean imports.

## 11. Implementation Stop Conditions

Future implementation phases should stop when their defined deliverable is complete and tested.

Do not automatically continue into:

- PDF catalog import
- Website scraping
- Supplier order workflow
- Warehouse workflow
- AI image generation
- Website publishing
- SaaS tenant architecture

Those are separate future phases.

## 12. Final Recommendation

The Product Intelligence Center should be implemented as an upgrade layer around the current Product Library, Product Import Center, PIM, Product Knowledge Engine, and Product Price Engine.

The safest development sequence is:

1. Establish Product Intelligence API boundary.
2. Improve Product List and Product Detail into a practical backend UI.
3. Add AI-assisted import mapping and review.
4. Add reviewable AI Product Profile.
5. Add Product Intelligence context APIs for downstream AI modules.
6. Harden permissions, storage, tests, and PostgreSQL compatibility.

This keeps the current system reusable, avoids duplicate product data, and prepares Product Intelligence Center to become the product knowledge foundation for AI Sales OS V5.3 Lite.
