## 1. Current System Overview

Restaurant Setup Pro has evolved from a product/quote platform into the foundation of AI Sales OS V5.3 Lite.

The system already contains many useful foundations:

- Customer Intelligence
- Product Intelligence
- Opportunity Intelligence
- Sales Inquiry workflow
- Quote Builder
- PI Builder
- Order conversion
- Product Import Center
- Product Price Engine
- AI Business Brain
- AI Cost Control
- System Debug Center
- Basic Dashboard

The strongest current business workflow is:

```text
Customer / Inquiry
  ↓
AI / Rules Analysis
  ↓
Opportunity / Customer Intelligence
  ↓
Product Matching
  ↓
Quote Builder
  ↓
PI Builder
  ↓
Order
```

The main product risk is not lack of features. The main risk is too many partially overlapping features competing for attention.

The next stage should simplify around the core sales operating workflow.

## 2. Core Product Definition

### 2.1 Product Positioning

Restaurant Setup Pro AI Sales OS V5.3 Lite is:

A focused AI-assisted sales operating system for hospitality furniture companies.

It helps a company:

1. Understand customers.
2. Identify sales opportunities.
3. Match products to customer needs.
4. Build accurate quotes quickly.
5. Generate professional PI documents.
6. Convert quotes to orders.

It is not currently:

- A full CRM suite.
- A warehouse system.
- A supplier procurement system.
- A website publishing system.
- A general AI agent platform.
- A full SaaS tenant/billing platform.

### 2.2 Core Workflow

The core V5.3 Lite workflow should be:

```text
Customer
  ↓
AI Customer Intelligence
  ↓
Opportunity
  ↓
Product Matching
  ↓
Quote
  ↓
PI
  ↓
Order
```

This workflow is supported by the current architecture, but it needs simplification and better UI connection.

### 2.3 Source of Truth

Keep these source-of-truth boundaries:

| Business Area | Source of Truth |
| --- | --- |
| Customer and opportunity | Customer / Opportunity Intelligence |
| Product data | Product Library / Product Intelligence Center |
| Product variants and attributes | Product Foundation / PIM |
| Supplier cost and reference price | Product Price Engine |
| Quote calculation | Quote Builder |
| Customer-facing PI | PI Builder |
| Order snapshot | Order Management |
| AI execution and budget | AI Business Brain + AI Cost Control |

AI should assist, explain, draft, and recommend. AI should not silently overwrite source-of-truth records.

## 3. Core Business Module Review

### 3.1 Customer Intelligence

Business value:

High.

Customer Intelligence answers:

- Who is this customer?
- What type of customer are they?
- Are they valuable long-term?
- Is there a current buying opportunity?
- What should sales do next?

Current completion status:

MVP complete.

Current capabilities:

- Customer records.
- Customer value score.
- Buying opportunity score.
- Sales priority score.
- Purchase timing handling.
- Feedback panel.
- Priority view.
- AI/rules explanation through AI Business Brain foundation.

Recommendation:

Keep and simplify.

Development priority:

Priority 1.

Why:

Customer Intelligence is the beginning of the core AI Sales OS workflow. If this is not clear and usable, downstream product matching and quotes are less valuable.

Modify:

- Make customer card simpler.
- Focus on three scores:
  - Customer Value
  - Buying Opportunity
  - Sales Priority
- Avoid adding complex CRM campaign features.

Delay:

- Automatic lead discovery.
- External enrichment.
- Email/WhatsApp automation.
- Advanced learning models.

Very high.

Product Intelligence answers:

- What products can we sell?
- Which variants exist?

- Which products fit a customer need?
- What information is missing before Quote/PI?

- Attributes.
- Variants.
- Media.
- Supplier Excel import.

- Price Engine integration.

Priority 2.

## 7. Duplicate Feature Review

### 7.1 Customer Intelligence vs Opportunity Intelligence

Overlap:

- Customer scoring.
- Opportunity scoring.
- AI recommendation.
- Next action.

Decision:

Modify.

Recommended boundary:

- Customer Intelligence = profile, customer value, buying opportunity, sales priority.
- Opportunity Intelligence = action queue, handoff, drafts, follow-up state.

### 7.2 Product Intelligence vs Product Knowledge Engine

Overlap:

- Product readiness.
- Suitable store types/styles/features.
- AI summary/keywords.

Decision:

Keep both, but clarify boundary.

Recommended boundary:

- Product Intelligence Center = product master, variants, attributes, media, price context, AI-ready profile.
- Product Knowledge Engine = structured product relationships, use cases, cases, matching signals.

In UI, they should feel like one Product Intelligence Center, not two separate product systems.

### 7.3 Quote Builder vs AI Proposal Generator

Overlap:

- Recommended products.
- Package generation.
- Customer-facing output.

Decision:

Keep Quote/PI. Delay Proposal Generator.

Recommended boundary:

- Quote Builder = commercial calculation.
- PI Builder = official customer-facing document.
- AI Proposal Generator later = richer narrative proposal draft, not replacement for Quote/PI.

### 7.4 AI Product Content Factory vs Product Intelligence AI Knowledge

Overlap:

- Product descriptions.
- SEO/GEO.
- FAQ.
- Sales talking points.
- Product keywords.

Decision:

Modify and simplify.

Recommended boundary:

- Product Intelligence AI Knowledge tab should be the main place users see product AI knowledge.
- AI Product Content Factory should become the generation/review backend, not a daily workspace for everyone.

### 7.5 Dashboard vs Module-Specific Dashboards

Overlap:

- Product metrics.
- Opportunity metrics.
- Cost metrics.
- Debug metrics.

Decision:

Modify.

Recommended boundary:

- Main Dashboard = action-focused summary.
- Module dashboards = deeper review only when needed.
- Debug Center = system health only.

## 11. Development Priority Roadmap

### Priority 0 — Stabilize and Simplify Navigation

Goal:

Make the product easier to understand.

Work:

- Hide low-value technical screens from non-admins.
- Keep five core sales areas visible:
  - Customers / Intelligence
  - Opportunities
  - Product Intelligence
  - Quotes / PI
  - Orders
- Keep Debug Center admin-only.

Expected impact:

Higher usability and less confusion.

### Priority 1 — Sales Workflow Hardening

Goal:

Make the core money path reliable.

Core workflow:

```text
Customer → Intelligence → Product Matching → Quote → PI → Order
```

Work:

- Improve transition from customer/inquiry to product matching.
- Make selected products flow cleanly into Quote Builder.
- Keep Quote/PI calculation stable.
- Improve order snapshot visibility.

Expected impact:

Sales can complete real customer workflow faster.

### Priority 2 — Product Intelligence UI

Goal:

Make products usable for matching and quoting.

Work:

- Implement PIC-2 UI in phases:
  - Product Intelligence Dashboard.
  - Product List.
  - Product Detail tabs.
  - Import Review table.

Expected impact:

Product Manager/Owner can maintain quote-ready product data.

### Priority 3 — Customer/Opportunity Unification

Goal:

Make customer intelligence and opportunity queue feel like one workflow.

Work:

- Clarify Customer Intelligence vs Opportunity Intelligence.
- Reduce duplicate scoring UI.
- Create one daily sales priority list.

Expected impact:

Sales knows who to contact today and why.

### Priority 4 — AI-Assisted Product Matching

Goal:

Connect Customer Intelligence with Product Intelligence.

Work:

- Use Product Context API.
- Match products/categories/variants to customer needs.
- Show explainable recommendations.
- Require sales review before quote creation.

Expected impact:

AI begins to directly improve quotation speed and quality.

### Priority 5 — Dashboard Simplification

Goal:

Turn dashboard into action center.

Work:

- Owner view.
- Sales view.
- Product Manager view.
- Hide debug metrics from normal dashboard.

Expected impact:

Users see what to do next instead of seeing everything.

### Priority 6 — Real AI Provider Expansion

Goal:

Use real LLM only after workflows are stable.

Work:

- Provider adapter.
- Prompt templates.
- Cost control.
- Reviewable outputs.

Expected impact:

Improves recommendation quality without losing control.

## 13. Risks

### 13.1 Product Overload Risk

Risk:

The product may become too broad: CRM, PIM, quote system, AI platform, supplier workflow, warehouse, SaaS admin.

Mitigation:

Keep V5.3 Lite focused on sales workflow.

### 13.2 Duplicate Module Risk

Risk:

Customer Intelligence, Opportunity Intelligence, Product Intelligence, Product Knowledge, AI Content Factory, and Dashboard may overlap.

Mitigation:

Define clear module ownership and hide implementation details from normal users.

### 13.3 AI Complexity Risk

Risk:

AI provider/prompt/log/cost machinery may become visible product clutter.

Mitigation:

Keep AI infrastructure in admin/debug layers. Business users see recommendations, not machinery.

### 13.4 Supplier Data Leakage Risk

Risk:

Supplier cost and internal data could leak through product, quote, or AI context.

Mitigation:

Continue server-side redaction. Test Sales/VA responses and DOM output.

### 13.5 Quote/PI Regression Risk

Risk:

New product/product matching work could break the existing quote/PI workflow.

Mitigation:

Treat Quote/PI as protected core. Run regression tests after each change.

### 13.6 SaaS Prematurity Risk

Risk:

Building SaaS infrastructure too early could harden the wrong product shape.

Mitigation:

Delay tenant/billing until workflow is validated.
