## 1. Business Objective

Phase 2 should help the business understand two different questions:

1. Is this customer worth building a long-term relationship with?
2. Is this customer likely to create business soon?

These are not the same thing.

Example:

- A hospitality furniture distributor may have very high long-term customer value but no immediate buying signal.
- A small restaurant startup may have low long-term customer value but a high immediate buying opportunity.

The system must therefore separate:

- Customer Value Score
- Buying Opportunity Score
- Sales Priority Score

The combined output should help sales decide:

- who is strategically important
- who needs immediate action
- who should be nurtured
- who should be deprioritized
- why the AI recommends that action

## 2. Core Scoring Models

### 2.1 Customer Value Score

Purpose:

Evaluate long-term customer importance.

Business question:

> Is this customer worth building a relationship with?

Suggested score range:

- 0–100

Suggested factors:

| Factor | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Customer Type | Distributor, design firm, restaurant group, owner/operator, etc. |
| Industry Fit | How strongly the customer belongs to the target industry |
| Product Fit | How well the company’s products match customer needs |
| Business Model | Distributor, project buyer, chain brand, one-time buyer |
| Company Size | Store count, market presence, project capacity |
| Import Capability | Ability to import or buy internationally |
| Historical Relationship | Previous quotes, PIs, orders, replies, feedback |
| Long-Term Potential | Repeat purchase / multi-project / strategic relationship potential |

Important:

- Customer Value should not depend only on immediate purchase timing.
- Missing timing data must not reduce strategic customer value by itself.

### 2.2 Buying Opportunity Score

Purpose:

Evaluate short-term sales opportunity.

Business question:

> Is this customer likely to create business soon?

Suggested score range:

- 0–100

Suggested factors:

| Factor | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Project Signals | New store, remodel, expansion, project announcement |
| RFQ Signals | Quote request, product inquiry, package request, DDP question |
| Renovation Signals | Remodel, rebrand, temporary closure, interior update |
| Expansion Signals | New location, franchise opening, multi-store rollout |
| Contact Interaction | Email reply, WhatsApp reply, inquiry, meeting |
| Website/Social Signals | Future extension point only; do not implement crawler in V1 |
| Signal Freshness | How recent and credible the signal is |
| Confidence Level | High / Medium / Low based on evidence quality |

Important:

If timing data is unavailable, the system must not guess.

Required display:

```text
Purchase Timing: Unknown
Confidence: Low
```

### 2.3 Sales Priority Score

Purpose:

Recommend practical sales action priority.

Business question:

> Who should sales contact first today?

Suggested approach:

```text
Sales Priority Score =
  Customer Value Score × Strategic Weight
  +
  Buying Opportunity Score × Urgency Weight
  +
  Contactability Adjustment
  +
  Sales Assignment / Due Date Adjustment
```

Default V1 recommendation:

- Customer Value Weight: 45%
- Buying Opportunity Weight: 45%
- Contactability / Action Readiness: 10%

Industry templates may override weights.

Example:

```text
Company: ABC Hospitality Distributor
Customer Value: 92
Buying Opportunity: 40
Sales Priority: 65

AI Recommendation:
High-value strategic customer. Maintain relationship and monitor buying signals.
```

## 7. Data Flow

### 7.1 Customer Intelligence Run

```text
User clicks Run Customer Intelligence
        ↓
Permission check
        ↓
Load customer + industry template
        ↓
Build AI/rules context
        ↓
Run rule-based scoring first
        ↓
Estimate AI cost if AI explanation is needed
        ↓
Run through AI Business Brain
        ↓
Save customer intelligence profile
        ↓
Show reviewable result
        ↓
Human approves/rejects/edits
```

### 7.2 Buying Opportunity Run

```text
User clicks Run Opportunity Intelligence
        ↓
Load customer signals and source data
        ↓
Detect explicit project/RFQ/renovation/expansion/contact signals
        ↓
If no timing data:
    purchase timing = Unknown
    confidence = Low
        ↓
Calculate buying opportunity score
        ↓
Use AI Brain for explanation if needed
        ↓
Save opportunity intelligence profile
```

### 7.3 Sales Priority Recalculation

```text
Customer Value Score
        +
Buying Opportunity Score
        +
Contactability / due-date readiness
        ↓
Sales Priority Score
        ↓
Priority reason
        ↓
Sales queue display
```

### 7.4 Feedback Learning Flow

```text
Sales submits feedback
        ↓
Feedback stored
        ↓
Score history updated if applicable
        ↓
AI/rules summarize feedback patterns
        ↓
Create scoring adjustment recommendation
        ↓
Owner/Admin reviews
        ↓
Approved changes update scoring template
```

Critical rule:

No automatic model training or critical scoring rule changes in V1.

## 11. Risks and Limitations

### 11.1 Business Risks

Risk:

Users may confuse Customer Value with Buying Opportunity.

Mitigation:

- UI labels must clearly separate long-term value from short-term opportunity.
- Sales Priority must explain how it combines both.

Risk:

AI may appear too confident when data is missing.

Mitigation:

- Unknown timing must be explicitly displayed.
- Confidence must be Low when evidence is missing.
- AI explanations must mention missing data.

### 11.2 Technical Risks

Risk:

Existing `opportunity_score` may conflict with new dual-score model.

Mitigation:

- Keep legacy fields for compatibility.
- Add clearly named new fields.
- Document mapping and transition behavior.

Risk:

Configurable scoring templates can become too complex.

Mitigation:

- Start with one default Hospitality Furniture template.
- Keep UI simple.
- Allow advanced editing later.

Risk:

AI costs may grow during batch analysis.

Mitigation:

- Use rule-based scoring first.
- Require cost estimate/confirmation for batch AI.
- Cache results.
- Limit deep analysis to selected customers.

### 11.3 Data Risks

Risk:

Historical data may be incomplete or inaccurate.

Mitigation:

- Store source confidence.
- Treat historical data as learning context.
- Allow human review and correction.

Risk:

Feedback may be noisy.

Mitigation:

- Do not automatically change scoring rules.
- Store feedback history.
- Require approval for scoring adjustment recommendations.

### 11.4 Product Limitations in V1

V1 should not include:

- automatic model training
- automatic outreach
- external data provider integration
- website crawling
- autonomous customer creation
- supplier-cost exposure to Sales/VA
- quote/PI auto-generation from discovered leads

## 13. Summary

Phase 2 should create the foundation for an AI Customer & Opportunity Intelligence Engine.

The critical shift:

```text
Lead List
    ↓
Customer Value Intelligence
    +
Buying Opportunity Intelligence
    ↓
Sales Priority Recommendation
    ↓
Human Review + Sales Feedback
    ↓
Future AI Learning
```

The system should be practical for Restaurant Setup Pro now, but structured for future industries and SaaS use later.

The safest development path is:

- configurable industry templates
- deterministic rules first
- AI explanations through AI Business Brain
- Cost Control for every AI execution
- human review before official business changes
- no external integrations in V1
