## 1. Existing Reusable Components

The current implementation already contains the foundations required for Product Intelligence Center. PIC-1 should reuse these foundations and must not create a duplicate product system.

### Product Core

- `products`
- `product_categories`
- `system_tags`
- `product_tag_links`
- `media_assets`
- `product_media_links`
- `product_documents`

These remain the source of truth for product master records, product categories, product tags, and product media.

### Product Foundation / PIM

- `product_variants`
- `product_attribute_definitions`
- `product_attribute_values`
- `product_attribute_category_links`
- `product_attribute_options`
- `product_foundation_relationships`

These remain the source of truth for variants, dynamic attributes, category attribute templates, related products, and frequently bought together relationships.

### Product Intelligence / Knowledge

- Product Intelligence fields on `products`
- `product_knowledge_terms`
- `product_knowledge_links`
- `product_relationships`
- `product_case_links`
- `product_keywords`
- `project_cases`
- `ai_product_content_drafts`
- `ai_image_generation_tasks`

These provide the existing knowledge, readiness, SEO/GEO, AI product content, and image generation task foundations.

### Product Import

- `product_import_batches`
- `product_import_drafts`
- `product_import_assets`
- `product_import_errors`
- `import_jobs`
- `src/services/smart-product-import.mjs`

The current import foundation supports XLSX/CSV parsing, merged cells, bilingual headers, embedded image extraction, product/variant grouping, draft review, and approval into Product Library.

### Product Price Engine

- `product_price_rules`
- price/cost fields on `product_variants`
- pricing recalculation APIs
- quote item cost/reference snapshots

Product Price Engine remains responsible for deterministic commercial price calculation. Product Intelligence may expose a safe pricing summary, but it must not calculate or mutate final pricing outside this engine.

### Permission and Redaction Helpers

Existing reusable server-side helpers:

- `canManageProductLibrary(user)`
- `canViewSensitiveProductData(user)`
- `redactSensitiveProductData(value, user)`
- `redactImportBatch(batch, user)`
- `redactImportDraft(draft, user)`

PIC-1 should reuse these helpers to prevent supplier cost and confidential purchasing data from leaking to Sales/VA roles.

### Existing Product Readiness Helpers

Existing reusable functions include:

- product readiness calculation
- product knowledge summary
- `syncProductReadiness(id)`
- product dashboard metrics

PIC-1 should reuse readiness data and expose it through a new API boundary rather than creating a second scoring system.

## 5. Required Additions for PIC-1

PIC-1 requires a clean read-only Product Intelligence API boundary.

### Required New APIs

- `GET /api/product-intelligence/products`
- `GET /api/product-intelligence/products/:id`
- `GET /api/product-intelligence/products/:id/quality`
- `GET /api/product-intelligence/context/products/:id`

### Required Behavior

The APIs should:

- Reuse existing product tables.
- Reuse existing product readiness/knowledge helpers.
- Return image, product name, SKU, category, status, AI confidence if available, and data quality if available.
- Return basic information, attributes, variants, media, pricing summary, AI profile, and source information according to permissions.
- Redact supplier cost and confidential supplier fields for unauthorized roles.
- Prepare clean product context for future AI recommendation, Quote Builder, PI Builder, and Sales Assistant.
- Not call external AI providers.
- Not change products, variants, import records, prices, or frontend behavior.

## 6. Potential Conflicts

### A. Duplicate Product API Risk

Risk:

Creating Product Intelligence endpoints could accidentally become a second product system.

Control:

PIC-1 APIs must be read-only boundaries over existing Product Library/PIM/Price Engine data.

### B. Supplier Cost Leakage Risk

Risk:

Product detail and pricing summaries may expose supplier cost, supplier SKU, supplier notes, or source supplier data.

Control:

Use server-side redaction helpers. Do not depend on frontend hiding.

### C. Existing API Regression Risk

Risk:

Adding new routes could disrupt existing `/api/products` or import routes.

Control:

Add routes under `/api/product-intelligence/*` only. Existing route behavior must remain unchanged.

### D. Readiness Score Duplication Risk

Risk:

Creating a second quality score could conflict with current product readiness.

Control:

PIC-1 should use existing readiness and derive quality response from current product data. No new database scoring table is created.

### E. Pricing Ownership Risk

Risk:

Product Intelligence pricing summary could be mistaken as a pricing engine.

Control:

Pricing calculation remains in Product Price Engine. Product Intelligence only exposes current approved/reference pricing and status summaries.

### F. AI Integration Scope Risk

Risk:

PIC-1 could expand into live AI mapping or profile generation.

Control:

PIC-1 has no external AI provider calls. It prepares context shape only.

## 7. PIC-0 Conclusion

The current system is ready for PIC-1 without schema changes.

PIC-1 should implement a read-only Product Intelligence API boundary using the existing Product Library, PIM, Product Import, Product Knowledge, and Product Price Engine foundations.

No duplicate product tables are required for PIC-1.
